How it works
  1. Search any market
    Type a keyword, company, agency, or NAICS code. In seconds you’ll see the total obligated value, who’s buying, who’s winning, and what’s expiring. This is a literal database, not Google, so keep terms exact. Use AND to intersect (e.g., DHS AND Splunk), commas for OR (Cloud, Cyber).
  2. Read the signals
    The Market Signals view shows you where the money is concentrated, which contracts are expiring, and which accounts to target. Pipeline Patterns are generated automatically from the same data — surfacing bridge contracts, recurring requirements, and follow-on adjacencies ranked by urgency. The data comes directly from USASpending. Govhoo simply adds an analysis and visualization layer so the market picture is easier to see.
  3. Get Arti’s take
    Tap Ask Arti for an AI strategy brief that reads the data, incumbents, entry points, teaming partners, and recommends what to do next.
Understanding the numbers
Why totals look high
The dollar figures reflect the full lifetime obligated value of multi-year contracts, not a single year's spend. Because Govhoo analyzes the top 100 largest contracts in your search, these numbers represent the heaviest hitters in your territory rather than the entire database.
What’s included
Govhoo strictly shows active contracts (end dates in the future) that have recorded funding actions in the past 12 months. This ensures your pipeline is built on active requirements and free of old, dead close-out contracts.
Where the data comes from
Every search queries the USASpending.gov API v2 in real time. There is no cache and no stale snapshots. Dollar amounts and dates are shown exactly as reported by federal agencies. Govhoo normalizes vendor names and resolves agency office hierarchies for readability, but never changes the underlying financial data. Your searches are not sent to Govhoo servers — recent searches are stored locally in your browser for convenience, and we use Google Analytics for basic site traffic.
What this isn’t
Govhoo is not an opportunity tracker or an RFP database. It is market intelligence that helps you quickly see how a federal market is structured so your team can decide where to focus.

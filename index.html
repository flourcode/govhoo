Getting Started
  1. Size Federal Markets
    Search a keyword, NAICS code, company, or agency to instantly see total spend, who's buying, and who's winning. Use AND to narrow — 541512 AND Air Force. Use a , to broaden — Cloud, Cyber. Shorthand like DHS, VA, DISA works too.
  2. Follow the Money
    See exactly how dollars move from agencies to primes, and which sub-agencies control the budget. Click any bar to drill in. Filter by NAICS code or contract vehicle to zero in on your target market.
  3. Find Partners
    See which primes have the broadest agency reach in your market, ideal teaming partners. Click any vendor to see their full federal portfolio. Switch to the Sub view to map prime→sub workshare relationships and find who actually does the work.

Built by a Sales Guy

Hi, I’m Mark. I’ve sold at AWS, F5, and Red Hat, served as a DISA COTR, and spent 20 years in the Marines. And even with all that government experience, I still wasted hours every month as a seller digging through USASpending, SAM.gov, and industry days just to understand where the money was flowing and who really owned the workshare.

I built Govhoo to help sales teams quickly decide whether a federal market is worth their time. It uses live USASpending data to show who owns the work, where the money flows, and whether spending is real or episodic. It doesn’t replace GovWin or BGOV. It helps you focus, so you can spend more time helping your customers. And it’s free.

How to use this for pipeline planning
You're looking at live federal award data from USASpending.gov. Every contract shown had funding activity in the past 12 months. Dollar values are Total Obligations to Date; the government's cumulative commitment over the life of the contract.

What each panel tells you
Sankey — where the money flows. Click any agency to drill into sub-agencies and see who controls the budget. Bubble Chart — market concentration at a glance. See if spend is spread across many vendors or locked up by a few. NAICS — which service categories carry the most investment. Click any row to narrow your search to that code. Prime Reach — primes ranked by how many agencies they serve. These are your strongest teaming candidates. Top Agencies — the buyers, ranked by spend. Click to drill into sub-agency breakdown. Vehicles — filter everything to a single contract vehicle to see who's on it and what's flowing through it.

How to use this for teaming & partnering
This is subcontracting activity from the past 12 months. Dollar amounts are individual transaction values, not cumulative obligations. Use this view to understand who actually does the work, who the gatekeepers are, and where teaming relationships already exist.

What each panel tells you
Sankey — how sub dollars flow from agencies through primes to subs. See which primes control the most workshare. Bubble Chart — market concentration. Toggle between primes and subs to see who dominates each side. Top Primes — who's awarding the most sub work. These are potential teaming partners if you're a sub, or competitors if you're a prime. Top Subs — who's receiving the most sub work, and which primes they work under. Find potential teammates or assess incumbent strength.

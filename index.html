How it works
  1. Size Your Market
    Search a keyword, NAICS code, company, or agency to instantly see total spend, who's buying, and who's winning. Use AND to narrow — 541512 AND Air Force. Use a , to broaden — Cloud, Cyber. Shorthand like DHS, VA, DISA works too.
  2. Find Your Target Accounts
    The treemap and Target Accounts panel rank agencies by opportunity quality — not just spend, but recompete pipeline, vendor fragmentation, mission-gap urgency, and program activity. Tap any target to drill into that agency's spend, vendors, and expiring contracts within your market.
  3. Get Arti's Strategic Read
    Tap Ask Arti for an AI-powered strategy brief grounded in your live market data. Arti identifies channel plays, expiring contracts to target, relevant industry conferences, and the exact next steps, then follow up with questions about your specific accounts.

How to read this data ›
You're looking at live federal award data from USASpending.gov. Every contract shown had funding activity in the past 12 months. Dollar values are total obligations to date — the government's cumulative commitment over the life of the contract.

Treemap — market concentration at a glance. Size = spend. Tap to drill into agencies, offices, and vendors. Target accounts — agencies ranked by opportunity quality: spend, recompete pipeline, vendor fragmentation, urgency, and program activity. Sankey — where the money flows from agencies to primes. Tap any bar to drill in. NAICS — which service categories carry the most investment. Tap to search. Prime reach — vendors ranked by agency breadth, your strongest teaming candidates. Ask Arti — AI strategy brief grounded in your live data, with conference intel and channel plays.

Search tips: Use AND with codes: 541512 AND Air Force. Commas broaden: Cloud, Cyber. Tap any NAICS or vendor name to see their full footprint.

How to read this data ›
This is subcontracting activity from the past 12 months. Dollar amounts are individual transaction values, not cumulative obligations. Use this to understand who does the work, who the gatekeepers are, and where teaming relationships already exist.

Sankey — how sub dollars flow from agencies through primes to subs. Bubble chart — concentration. Toggle between primes and subs. Top primes — who's awarding the most sub work — teaming partners or competitors. Top subs — who's receiving the most sub work and which primes they serve under.

Tap any vendor name to see their full federal footprint. Use the People/Jobs/News links to find contacts.

AND searches: Subcontract data shows the broader market. The USASpending subaward API does not support AND filtering. Use the Prime view for precise AND-filtered totals.

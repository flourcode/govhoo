How Govhoo Works

Live federal contract data from USASpending.gov, turned into the market intelligence that FPDS ezSearch never had.

Search anything

Type a keyword, company name, NAICS code, or agency into the search bar. Govhoo figures out what you mean and pulls live data from the government's spending database.

Use AND to narrow results — 541512 AND Air Force finds IT services contracts awarded by the Air Force. Use commas to broaden — Cloud, Cyber, AI searches all three.

Agency shorthand like DHS, DoD, VA, and DISA automatically filters to that specific agency, not just awards mentioning the name.

Under the hood

For the technically curious.

Data source & API

Every search queries the USASpending.gov API v2 in real time — no cache, no stale snapshots.

Endpoints

/api/v2/search/spending_by_award/ — Primary contract search returning individual award records.

/api/v2/search/spending_by_category/naics — NAICS breakdown (the award endpoint doesn't return NAICS reliably).

Subawards use the same award endpoint with subawards: true, limited to 12 months by the API.

Prime awards cover the trailing 5 federal fiscal years.

Search parsing logic

Term classification

6-digit numbers → NAICS code filter. PSC patterns (D302, R425) → PSC filter. Agency shorthand (DHS, DoD) → structured agency filter. Everything else → keyword search.

AND operator

Uppercase AND or + creates an intersection. Each part is classified independently — 541512 AND Air Force applies a NAICS filter AND an agency filter. Lowercase "and" stays in the phrase.

Smart cleanup

Legal suffixes (LLC, Inc., Corp.) are stripped before parsing so company names with commas don't split into multiple terms.

Active contract filtering

The API returns any contract with an action in the date window, including $0 closeout mods. Govhoo removes contracts ended 90+ days ago unless modified within 30 days (indicating a likely extension). Vehicle types and set-aside codes are detected by pattern matching.

Mission gap scoring

Expiring contracts are scored on time remaining, dollar value, operational signals (keywords like "maintenance," "hosting," "cyber," "O&M"), and PSC service family.

High: <180 days, 2+ operational signals, >$250K. Medium: <365 days, operational, >$100K. Low: everything else.

Target account ranking

Top 5 agencies scored on recompete pipeline value, high-risk contract count, vendor fragmentation (fewer vendors = less competition), and total spend. Recompete value and urgency are weighted more heavily than raw spend.

Pipeline generation

Targets assembled from three sources: expiring contracts (recompetes grouped by office), recurring spend (offices with repeat awards), and adjacent opportunities (offices buying related services). Each target includes close date, amount, incumbent, NAICS, and rationale.

Privacy

No account required, no searches stored, no individual tracking beyond anonymous analytics. All data is public federal spending data. The AI feature sends search context to a hosted model but retains nothing between sessions.
